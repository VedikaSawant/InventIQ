Inventory Optimization Fundamentals

Inventory management aims to maintain the right stock levels
to meet customer demand while minimizing costs.

There are two primary costs:

1. Holding Cost
This is the cost of storing inventory.
Examples include storage space, insurance, and spoilage.

2. Stockout Cost
This occurs when demand cannot be met due to insufficient stock.
Stockouts lead to lost sales and customer dissatisfaction.

Demand Forecasting

Demand forecasting predicts future product demand using
historical sales data.

Machine learning models such as LSTM networks can identify
temporal demand patterns.

Reinforcement Learning in Inventory

Reinforcement learning agents learn optimal ordering policies
by interacting with an environment.

The agent observes:

- Current stock level
- Forecasted demand
- Past inventory actions

Then decides:

- How many units to order

The objective is to minimize:

- Holding costs
- Stockout penalties

Explainable AI

SHAP explanations identify which factors most influenced
a decision.

For example:

- Low stock levels increase order quantity
- High predicted demand increases order quantity
- High holding costs decrease order quantity
